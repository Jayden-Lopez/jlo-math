🎯 What IS an Inequality?

An equation says two things are EQUAL (like x = 5).
An inequality says one thing is bigger or smaller than another — like a range of answers instead of just one!

🏟️ Soccer analogy: "Our team needs MORE THAN 3 goals to win" → that's an inequality! Not just exactly 3 — anything more than 3 works: 4, 5, 10, 100...
>
Greater Than
x > 5 means x is MORE than 5
(6, 7, 8... but NOT 5)
<
Less Than
x < 5 means x is LESS than 5
(4, 3, 2... but NOT 5)
≥
Greater Than OR Equal
x ≥ 5 means 5 OR MORE
(5, 6, 7... YES, 5 counts!)
≤
Less Than OR Equal
x ≤ 5 means 5 OR LESS
(5, 4, 3... YES, 5 counts!)
🦈 Memory trick — The alligator eats the BIGGER number!
The open mouth > or < always points toward the smaller number. So 8 > 3 — the mouth opens toward 8 (the bigger one)!
📏 Graphing on a Number Line

Number lines show ALL the answers at once. Two key rules:

🔴 Open circle (○) = that number is NOT included — use with > or <
🟡 Filled circle (●) = that number IS included — use with ≥ or ≤
Example: x > 3 → open circle, shade to the RIGHT
Example: x ≤ 5 → filled circle, shade to the LEFT
⚽ Quick rule:
• > or ≥ → shade to the RIGHT (bigger numbers)
• < or ≤ → shade to the LEFT (smaller numbers)
🔑 How to SOLVE an Inequality

It's almost exactly like solving an equation — just keep the sign instead of =

1
Write the inequality: x − 9 < 14
(This is problem 21 from your test!)
2
Do the same thing to both sides to get x alone.
Add 9 to both sides: x − 9 + 9 < 14 + 9
3
Simplify: x < 23 ✅
That's your answer! Any number less than 23 works.
4
Graph it: Open circle at 23, shade LEFT (smaller numbers)
⚠️ ONE SPECIAL RULE: If you multiply or divide by a NEGATIVE number, FLIP the sign!
Example: −x < 5 → divide by −1 → x > −5 (sign flipped!)
